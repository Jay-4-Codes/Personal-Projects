Creamy No-Bake Peach Tart

Description

This creamy no-bake peach tart is a refreshing dessert perfect for warm weather.

Ingredients

  • 9 graham crackers
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds
  • 8 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons honey

Steps

  1. Line a 9-inch tart pan with a round of parchment paper. Lightly grease the pan and paper.
  2. Prepare the crust: place graham crackers, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt into a food processor. Process until fine crumbs form.
  3. Toast almonds in a skillet for about 5 minutes. Add to food processor and pulse with crumbs.
  4. Melt butter and add to the food processor with honey. Pulse until combined.
  5. Press crust mixture into the tart pan and freeze for 15 minutes.
  6. For filling, combine gelatin and cold water in a saucepan. Let stand for 5 minutes.
  7. Add peaches, sugar, lemon juice, vanilla, yogurt, and cream to a blender. Blend until smooth.
  8. Dissolve gelatin over low heat, then blend with the peach mixture.
  9. Pour peach filling into the tart crust and refrigerate for at least 6 hours.
  10. Garnish with peaches and blueberries before serving, if desired.

Afang Soup: A Green, Leafy Nigerian Vegetable Soup

Description

Afang soup is a popular Nigerian dish made with Afang leaves and waterleaf, commonly eaten by the Efik and Ibibio people.

Ingredients

  • 400g sliced Okazi/Afang leaves
  • 250g water leaves
  • 20-25cl red palm oil
  • Beef, Kanda, and dry fish
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • Pepper and salt (to taste)
  • 2 stock cubes

Steps

  1. Boil beef and Kanda with diced onions and stock cubes. Add dry fish and cook for 5 more minutes.
  2. Add palm oil, crayfish, and pepper. Once boiling, add Afang and water leaves.
  3. Let the soup simmer for 5 minutes. Serve hot with fufu or eba.

Akara (Beans Cake)

Description

Akara is a popular West African fritter made from peeled beans, seasoned, and deep-fried in small balls.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups black-eyed or Nigerian brown beans
  • 1 small red onion
  • Hot spicy pepper (Habanero, Scotch Bonnet, or cayenne)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • 1 to 1.5 sweet peppers (optional)

Before You Prepare Akara

  • Soak beans for 5 minutes, then peel them.
  • Soak peeled beans for at least one hour to soften.
  • Chop onions and peppers.
  • For colored Akara, chop the sweet peppers.

Steps

  1. Remove soaked beans from water and grind in small batches with little water.
  2. For colored Akara, add chopped sweet peppers to the beans before blending.
  3. Rest blended beans for 30 minutes to one hour to activate oligosaccharides.
  4. Whisk beans paste until fluffy and white.
  5. Heat oil for deep frying. Add salt, onions, and spicy pepper to the paste.
  6. Scoop paste into hot oil. Fry until golden brown.
